Hazelwood vs. Kuhlmeier

In this case, a principal pulled several articles from a school newspaper that related to divorce and teen pregnancy, which the students protested against. It was eventually ruled that the principal had the right to do so because school papers have their own distinct purpouse, and the school has a final say in what that purpouse is. The students felt that their right to free press was being violated as their articles were being censored, but in reality they were part of a private press.
